# Commentary

This model depicts the AISC Steel Sculpture, a physical teaching aid which has been installed more than 170 times at universities and colleges around the world. The sculpture is a full size 3D model of 25 steel members and 43 distinct connections, which give engineering students an opportunity for hands on experience of these assemblies

This website contains digital models of the sculpture, as well as 10 of it's unique connections. These connections were selected by an industry survey of AEC professionals as the most realevant to their practice. In addition to the models, sample calculations for the design of each individual connection are included. Through these resources students will be able to connect the physical assembly to a BIM model to the calculations learned in class.

The AISC steel sculpture contributions to this website were developed by Samuel Leman, Sophia Mitchell, and Samuel Nash at Gonzaga University. These contributions are a part of a senior design project sponsored by the American Institute of Steel Construction.
